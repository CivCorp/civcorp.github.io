CivCorp FAQ

How do I lease a plot?

Plots are put up for auction every few months. The winner gets exclusive use of the plot until the lease expires.

At least one week before the lease ends, the city will start an auction so that the leaseholder loses the auction, they will have time to clear the plot before the new leaser arrives. If the plot is not cleared before then, the city may acid-block the plot.

Once you own a plot, you may build whatever you want and you can reinforce it to your own groups.

How do I sign legal documents?

Because written signatures can be forged, CivCorp uses digital signatures to sign all legal documents. Digital signatures look something like the following.

-------BEGIN SIGNATURE------

IQB1AwUBMVSiA5QYCuMfgNYjAQFAKgL/ZkBfbeNEsbthba4BlrcnjaqbcKgNv+
a5kr4537y8RCd+RHm75yYh5xxA1ojELwNhhb7cltrp2V7LlOnAelws4S87UX80c
LBtBcN6AACf11qymC2h+Rb2j5SU+rmXWru+=QFMx

------END SIGNATURE------

The mathematics are complex, but the important part to understand is that digital signatures are mathematically impossible to forge, and they can be decrypted to see who signed them.

Install Keybase

To create and verify digital signatures we use Keybase. Setting up Keybase requires a few steps.

  1. Download and install Keybase.
  2. If you are using a Mac, open up Terminal and run keybase pgp gen. This will set up your PGP keys which are used to sign documents.

Sign documents with Keybase

To sign documents do the following:

  1. Copy the exact content of the document to your clipboard.
  2. If you are using a Mac, open up Terminal and run pbpaste | keybase pgp sign | pbcopy.
  3. Edit the signature into the document where a signature would normally go.

Identity proofs

How do we know that a username on Keybase actually is a person in-game, and not an impostor?

For example, how do we know "thelonetopwolfx" is "TheLoneTopWolfx" on CivRealms and that "TheLoneTopWolfx" is "thelonetopwolfx" on Keybase? The most certain solution involves two steps:

  1. Have "thelonetopwolfx" on Keybase sign a message saying "I am TheLoneTopWolfx on CivRealms."
  2. Have "TheLoneTopWolfx" in CivRealms sign a book (using a Book and Quill) saying "I am thelonetopwolfx on Keybase.", and have him distribute copies to interested parties as necessary.

Since neither of these can be forged, these two actions prove that the same person controls both accounts.
